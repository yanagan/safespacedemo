Ashley, 19, discusses hitting rock bottom

Hard times

I was the normal happy easy going 19 year old male. I had fun with my mates and loved my girlfriend, but that all changed I went in to hospital with kidney stones and that night my girlfriend went out with some guys from work cos she didn't know I was sick and things happened. All because she thought I didn't care about her anymore. So we broke up, but I still loved her so I worked my butt off and got her back. Then I lost my job and I lost my 14 day old nephew and my pop in the same week, but I was okay cos I had my girlfriend/best friend to help me through it.

But on the 18th of this month she left me with no reason. She just said she was over me and us. And I went downhill from there, not eating, self harming, not seeing my friends, or really living life. I didn't understand why she did it or how she could. After 2 and a half years, I was lost and hurting bad.

Nothing anyone could say was helping, so I saw a doctor and he helped a little bit by telling me to try and change my life. It was hard and the days went forever and the nights were worse, but life is slowly getting better.

I know it's hard - trust me, it is really hard - but the best thing to try it is talk about it with someone. A doctor is good cos they can help and it is going to take me a very long time to get over her. I miss her and still want to talk to her, but I know if I wanna get over her, I can't do it.

I just hope this story helps anyone who needs it and there's no real advice I can give because I'm still going through it myself, but hey, everyone says life gets better" so I guess we just have to let it happen. That's what I'm trying to do and I hope if you're having a real hard time, you get help and try it too. Life sucks, but when you hit rock bottom the only way is up. Thanks headspace.
