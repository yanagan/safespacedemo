Brittany, 16, discusses depression and self-harm

One day I had a dream that changed almost everything for me.I'd been feeling depressed for a while, although I hardly recognised this. I was home from school, and was working on a history essay, but I couldn't concentrate. I researched depression instead, because I knew that something was wrong with me, although there was no reason for anything to be wrong. I was exhausted, and had a nap. I dreamt that I was self-harming. Even worse, in my dream I was enjoying it. Somehow, it made me feel almost better. More alive. More real. Before I'd feel worse. That was the 8/8/08. It was the first day of the Beijing Olympics; the first day I self-harmed. The next six months were the worst. I struggled constantly to not self-harm. It wasn't really sadness I felt, just nothingness and badness. Sadness, at least, is a clear emotion. Nothingness and badness is not. It was like a Dementor had kissed me. I found out as much information as I could about self-harm, depression and the like, because to me, knowledge is power, and I wanted power more than anything. I felt like I knew where I was and where I wanted to go, but not how to get there. I â€˜had' to fight on my own. I didn't belong with my friends, and felt out of place at school every single day. People sometimes asked if I was okay, but I always replied that I was tired. I later figured this was because I didn't really want any proof that it was real, and school was my escape. My closest friends thought I was bubbly, happy and funny-they told me in life skills once that these were my strengths. I got straight A's. I was good at sport. I had a boyfriend. My life seemed perfect. But I still felt terrible. And while I hated the fact that I self harmed, and hid it, there were times, where I didn't even feel like I belonged with 'real' self-harmers. I had this crazy idea in my head that as I never hurt my arms, I didn't belong. I only ever hurt my knees, ankles, and hips, and was always fighting to stop myself. That was the only way I didn't become completely addicted. I haven't self-harmed since the 13/3/09. I felt occasionally depressed for a while, and I'll always be healing. At one stage late in 2009, I became worried that one of my friends was depressed. While I was wrong, this fear was what led me to contact Kids Help Line. I emailed them and didn't receive a reply for weeks, but when I did, I felt amazing. I felt this invisible weight lift from my shoulders and I was free from self harm and depression. My counsellor, who I emailed regularly, helped me formulate answers to questions I didn't know how to ask, and helped me to heal my final wounds. Contacting Kids Help Line gave me a safety net that I desperately needed, and helped me gather the strength to tell my mother. I wrote her a letter. Having people know has made all the difference. I fought. I survived. I am healing. But the process would have been faster and less painful if I had have sought help sooner. Fight, survive, heal but not alone.
