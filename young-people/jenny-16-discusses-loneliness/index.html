Jenny, 16, discusses loneliness

Trusting Loneliness

So my story starts at six. My family was rough, living on government funding and struggling for everyday needs.

At the age six, my dad was taken away for hurting me and my family. After that, my mum struggled to find stability in life, constantly on the edge, scared of what was to become of us. Trying different men and different towns to find something that made her happy.

By 12, I had been to 12 schools, had five "fathers" and 14 houses. At the age of 13, I battled an unwanted relationship with a man a lot older than me that ended in unwanted events. After a while, things began to bundle up. I began realising what life was for me and what it had made me into. I began physical harm, esteem issues, and depression. I felt alone and felt I couldn't trust anyone again... they were all scary to me.

I hit year nine and my grades started to fall... I stopped talking, began closing off from the world in fear of what might happen and what did happen. Until the middle of year ten when I met someone I looked up to and I trusted every word that left their mouth: a local youth worker. I was able to open up to them they guided me every step of the way. It even meant seeking professional help. I had the whole set (psychologists, psychiatrists and co.) Yeah, it's scary, but it's worth the ride... things aren't one hundred percent yet, but things are improving and will continue to improve as long as I continue to have hope.
