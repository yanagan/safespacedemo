headspace Maroochydore is a partnership of local health and community service organisations with an aim to provide fully integrated primary health care services for young people in one “youth friendly” environment.

To achieve this aim, headspace Maroochydore seeks a General Practitioner to operate out of their modern site centrally located in Evans Street, Maroochydore.  General Practice services will complement and enhance the provision of early intervention treatment in mental health for young people within the region by providing Mental Health Care Plans, sexual and general health checks and education of suicide prevention.

 

What we can offer?

What we require from you?

  • A commitment to at least one session a week (allowing you to continue your involvement in your usual practice);
  • A desire to make a difference in the lives of young people;
  • Provide a full range of health services including sexual and reproductive health, chronic health management and general health checks;
  • Be Vocationally Registered and eligible to apply for a Medicare Provider number

EOI - Private Practice Opportunities for Allied Health Professionals, Part-time/Sessional (Click to show below) (Click to hide below)

Private Practice opportunities for Allied Health Professionals with headspace Chatswood and headspace Brookvale

headspace Chatswood is seeking expressions of interest from Clinical Psychologists, Psychologists, Social Workers and Occupational Therapists who are experienced and interested in providing high quality services to young people aged 12 – 25 years old. headspace Chatswood Allied Health Professionals will provide services under the early intervention / prevention framework for young people with emerging or recent onset mental health problems.  headspace Chatswood provides a comprehensive suite of services that include mental health care, primary health care, drug and alcohol and vocational services.  All services are at no cost to the young person.

Working as a sessional professional at  headspace Chatswood provides you with the opportunity to work in a youth-friendly environment.  A modest room rental/administrative fee will be charged and in return you will receive extensive clinical and administrative support, as well as on-going referrals of young people who have been assessed and are ready to receive services.  There are opportunities to add to a learning environment along with the headspace staff and other professionals that are co-located at headspace Brookvale. headspace Chatswood is a dynamic, innovative and multi-disciplinary environment.

Essential Criteria:

  • A degree in a recognised Allied Health profession with current full AHPRA registration
  • Demonstrated experience providing therapy to young people aged 12 to 25 years old
  • Eligibility to provide services under Medicare and be willing to Bulk Bill
  • An ABN
  • Professional Indemnity ($10 million or above) and Public Liability Insurance ($10 million or above)
  • Commitment to be an integrated part of a multidisciplinary team
  • Able to use headspace Chatswood and headspace Brookvale record-keeping and HAPI data collection systems

headspace Chatswood and headspace Brookvale is committed to a high standard of service delivery and the nature of the position will require:

  • A Working with Children Check
  • A Criminal Records Check
  • Evidence of completion of a Child Protection Training within the last three years or willingness to complete a free training

Desirable Criteria:

  • Ability to provide afternoon after-school appointments
  • Ability to speak a second language

EOI - General Practitioner Private Provider, Part-time/Sessional (Click to show below) (Click to hide below)

Deadline: 01/07/2016 12:00am

Applications are invited from suitably qualified medical practitioners eligible for registration in New South Wales for the above position within headspace Chatswood and headspace Brookvale, Northern Sydney Local Health District (NSLHD).
Applicants must be currently registered with the Medical Board of Australia and have current membership with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA). If successful, the applicant will not be subsequently eligible to commence their appointment until after the relevant qualifications and requirements are formally completed.
headspace, the National Youth Mental Health Foundation Ltd, is an Australian government funded, not-for profit organisation committed to improving the lives of young people aged 12-25 years. 
Each headspace centre is a youth friendly place where young people can access low cost or free help, for a range of health and wellbeing problems. Services are provided by a variety of professionals working in each centre including youth workers, allied mental health workers, GPs and Psychiatrists.
headspace is an early intervention service, so high prevalence disorders including anxiety and depression form the majority of presentations. In addition GPs are encouraged and supported to engage young people around sexual health, drug and alcohol, other psychical health problems as well as preventative health care.

Working at a headspace centre gives GP’s an opportunity to: 
• Improve the lives of young people
• Provide a full range of health services, with an emphasis on managing mental health issues
• Be part of a supportive, multidisciplinary team 
• Develop confidence and increased skills in working with young people
• Appointment management, bulk billing and practice management support enable the GP to focus on delivering high quality medical services to young people
• Access to onsite Private Allied Health Professional who provide Psychological Services under Medicare and ATAPS
• Smooth referral pathways into on site co-located providers, including Psychiatrists, Drug and Alcohol counsellors, Youth Health Nurse, vocational consultants, and youth homelessness case workers (Brookvale)
• Continuing Professional Development in field of Adolescent and Young Person health and mental health
• Access to headspace National Office GP Advisor

Other benefits include:
• Supervision and training including engaging young people, youth mental health and the headspace model of care; 
• Focus on evidence based practice– from assessment to appropriate prescribing;
• Working within a multidisciplinary team– with access to support and joint management of patients with the clinical team; 
• Modern fully computerised facilitates with full reception and admin support;

Employer: Grand Pacific Health

This is an exciting opportunity for an appropriately qualified and registered Mental Health Professional to work within a Suicide Prevention program that works with young people (12-25 years) who are at risk of suicide or are self-harming. This program has been showcased nationally and internationally for its innovation and excellence, as well as the way in which the program involves supporting the young person’s family. 

We require an enthusiastic, dedicated professional who is a strong communicator and keen to work collaboratively with a team. You will be an approved Medicare provider and have experience in the assessment and diagnosis of psychological disorders and provide evidence-based psychological therapies to adult clients, and young clients aged 12 years and over. 

This program is funded under the Access to Allied Psychological Services (ATAPS) initiative, which is a targeted program designed to provide psychological treatments to hard to reach groups. 

A demonstrated understanding of suicide and self-harm prevention, particularly related to the health and wellbeing needs of young people is required.
