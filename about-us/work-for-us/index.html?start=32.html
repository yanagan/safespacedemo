General Practitioner, Part-time/Sessional (Click to show below) (Click to hide below)

Employer: Wesley Mission Brisbane

GP Practitioner, Contract, one session of four (4) hours per week

headspace Hervey Bay is seeking a motivated and experienced General Practitioner with an interest in youth mental health.

headspace Hervey Bay provides assistance to young people aged 12 – 25 years. 

Our Centre is extending its available services to include a GP clinic for one morning or afternoon (preferably afternoon) per week.  The period of contract will be to 30th June 2016.

Working in our bulk-billing centre, the General Practitioner will provide –

  • An opportunity for young people to access help earlier through GP appointments
  • Early identification, diagnosis and treatment of physical and mental health problems
  • Youth-friendly medical service, which is welcoming of family involvement
  • Mental health treatment planning and review
  • Services that are respectful, non-judgemental and respect diversity.

headspace Hervey Bay will provide the GP with –

  • A fixed hourly wage
  • Consultation and treatment rooms in an attractive centrally located office
  • Administration staff for appointment management and practice management
  • A collaborative work-environment with experiencedstaff and a team-approach to the assessment and treatment of youth mental health problems
  • Supervision/guidance with delivery of psycho-social interventions for psychological problems (if desired)
  • Participation in team reviews of clients
  • A paper-less computer patient-information system
  • Smooth care-coordination with on-site services of psycho-social treatments (individual & skills groups), MBS-funded allied health practitioners, monthly tele-medicine clinic by a Child & Youth Psychiatry Consultant, and a twice monthly sexual health clinic conducted by Qld Health

The GP will need to hold the necessary registrations and insurances to operate a General Practice clinic.

To express your interest in this position, please contact Gerry Howe, Manager, on telephone 4303 2100.

Allied Mental Health Private Practitioner, Part-time/Sessional (Click to show below) (Click to hide below)

Salary: Contract

headspace Albany is seeking experienced Allied Mental Health Practitioners (Clinical or Registered Psychologists, Social Workers or Occupational Therapists) to work with young people aged 12-25 and their families, to deliver focussed psychological strategies at the  headspace Albany Centre.

You must be AHPRA/AASW Registered as a specialist Allied Mental health professional, and be eligible for Medicare Rebates. You must also have Professional indemnity insurance, an ABN and a current WWC Check card.

You must also be willing, and ideally experienced in working a multidisciplinary team. Our office hours are Monday – Friday 9- 5, and 9 – 7pm on Wednesdays. There is some flexibility for different session times across the week, and we are particularly interested in Clinicians to expand our  Wednesday evening service.

headspace Albany offers administrative support including bulk- billing appointment bookings and reminders, comfortable youth-friendly counselling rooms, and an ongoing base of referrals. Care coordination, case conferencing, access to integrated GP and Psychiatry services and professional development opportunities are available.

EOI - General Practitioner Private Provider, Part-time/Sessional (Click to show below) (Click to hide below)

Deadline: 01/07/2016 12:00am

Applications are invited from suitably qualified medical practitioners eligible for registration in New South Wales for the above position within headspace Chatswood and headspace Brookvale, Northern Sydney Local Health District (NSLHD).
Applicants must be currently registered with the Medical Board of Australia and have current membership with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA). If successful, the applicant will not be subsequently eligible to commence their appointment until after the relevant qualifications and requirements are formally completed.
headspace, the National Youth Mental Health Foundation Ltd, is an Australian government funded, not-for profit organisation committed to improving the lives of young people aged 12-25 years. 
Each headspace centre is a youth friendly place where young people can access low cost or free help, for a range of health and wellbeing problems. Services are provided by a variety of professionals working in each centre including youth workers, allied mental health workers, GPs and Psychiatrists.
headspace is an early intervention service, so high prevalence disorders including anxiety and depression form the majority of presentations. In addition GPs are encouraged and supported to engage young people around sexual health, drug and alcohol, other psychical health problems as well as preventative health care.

Working at a headspace centre gives GP’s an opportunity to: 
• Improve the lives of young people
• Provide a full range of health services, with an emphasis on managing mental health issues
• Be part of a supportive, multidisciplinary team 
• Develop confidence and increased skills in working with young people
• Appointment management, bulk billing and practice management support enable the GP to focus on delivering high quality medical services to young people
• Access to onsite Private Allied Health Professional who provide Psychological Services under Medicare and ATAPS
• Smooth referral pathways into on site co-located providers, including Psychiatrists, Drug and Alcohol counsellors, Youth Health Nurse, vocational consultants, and youth homelessness case workers (Brookvale)
• Continuing Professional Development in field of Adolescent and Young Person health and mental health
• Access to headspace National Office GP Advisor

Other benefits include:
• Supervision and training including engaging young people, youth mental health and the headspace model of care; 
• Focus on evidence based practice– from assessment to appropriate prescribing;
• Working within a multidisciplinary team– with access to support and joint management of patients with the clinical team; 
• Modern fully computerised facilitates with full reception and admin support;

For enquiries about the position please contact Kathi Boorman, Service Director Child and Youth Mental Health Services NSLHD at (02) 9887 5469 or Hema Setty, Medical Workforce Manager MHDA at 9887 5563.
